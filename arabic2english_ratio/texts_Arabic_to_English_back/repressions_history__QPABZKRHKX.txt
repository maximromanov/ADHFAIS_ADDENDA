

In the Middle Ages, Arabs lived in a period characterized by oppression and tyranny, where people were subjected to persecution and political repression. The oppressive regimes used many methods to control the people and establish their influence in governance.

The history of Arab oppression and tyranny consists of many chapters, as Arab countries witnessed powerful oppressive regimes from the Umayyad Caliphate period until the Abbasid era and beyond. This period was considered one of the most difficult for Arabs, as they suffered from despotism and brutality by rulers and security forces.

The Umayyad Caliphate system that prevailed in the seventh century was marked by continuous oppression, where Arabs were marginalized and any voice opposing the ruler's authority was criminalized. Violence and torture were used as a means to control the Arab people. This was synonymous with the suffering and injustice endured by the Arab people during that period.

During the Abbasid period, oppression and tyranny continued incessantly. Security forces suppressed any threat to their authority and recorded a history filled with injustices and repression. Security forces used torture and arbitrary detention to confront any opposition, and many intellectuals and thinkers who refused to comply with the existing political system were imprisoned.

The intensity of oppression and tyranny increased in the late Middle Ages, as Arab countries began to print the walls of caliphate with the blood of the innocent. The Arab people witnessed widespread violence and criminalization during that period, and tyrannical rulers used advanced methods of oppression to control the people and strengthen their influence.

Periods of oppression and tyranny continued in the Arab world until modern times. The Arab people witnessed periods of tyranny and despotism, and they were subjected to human rights violations and criminalization of any political opposition.

In the end, the history of the Arab world carries within it painful stories of oppression and tyranny. Despite the difficulties faced by the Arab people, they were able to overcome them and preserve their identity and rich culture. It is necessary for us to learn from those dark periods and work diligently to ensure human rights and basic freedoms for all individuals in our contemporary societies.
