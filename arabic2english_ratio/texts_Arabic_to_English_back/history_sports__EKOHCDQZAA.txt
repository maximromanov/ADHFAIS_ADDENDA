

The history of sports represents a intersection between culture and heritage, as sports are considered one of the physical activities that have persisted throughout ages and have been an essential part of people's lives. Sports have played a significant role in building civilizations and developing societies, and throughout history, famous sports and traditional games with a rich history have emerged.

In the Arab world, many traditional sports have been played, embodying the culture and heritage. Equestrianism is one of the most prominent of these sports, as the region has witnessed significant development in this field. For thousands of years, equestrianism has been a means of competition and entertainment, experiencing growth and popularity in the Middle Ages. Cities such as Cairo, Damascus, and Baghdad thrived thanks to this sport.

Equestrianism gained great importance in the Arab Islamic civilization, where it was one of the favorite sports of leaders and kings. It formed rituals and events held in Arab societies and received great appreciation.

In addition, combat sports are part of the history of Arab societies. During the Middle Ages, there were two main types of combat: personal defense arts and martial arts. These arts were taught for defense purposes as well as for leisure and sports. These arts spread in the Middle Ages and were reinforced in temples and schools for young people to learn.

We can see how sports are linked to history in the genesis of modern Olympic Games. Baron Pierre de Coubertin organized the first modern Olympic Games in 1896 in Greece, inspired by the ancient games that we consider historical. Since then, the Olympic Games have come to symbolize the spirit of sportsmanship and healthy competition between nations and peoples.

Sports and history have a close relationship, as sports are evident in the development of societies and their cultural influences. Sports are an integral part of human life, reflecting a rich and diverse history of our societies.
