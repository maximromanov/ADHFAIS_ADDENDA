

There is no doubt that travel is the key to liberation from the constraints of life, a means of discovering new cultures and soaring in the sky of freedom. However, sometimes travel is faced with the oppression and tyranny of oppressive systems. Historically, travel has been associated with some restrictions and oppression, as it is considered a tool for manipulating and controlling individuals; boycotting travel is considered a personal matter that can be exploited politically, which contributes to increasing oppression and tyranny.

The experience of travel is filled with stories of struggle and resistance against oppression. Freedom and protest against oppressive and oppressive systems were the main way to bring about change. For example, the boycott movement in the United States during the slavery period can be considered. When slaves had no rights, travel was a powerful tool for the struggle against the oppressive system. One prominent example is the Montgomery Bus Boycott and the Public Bus Movement, where famous black citizens boycotted public transportation due to their racial discrimination by the racist segregation system in the United States. These movements aimed to stop racial discrimination practices and ensure equality between black and white individuals in public transportation.

Oppression and tyranny in travel are not limited to racist societies only, but also extend to political systems that practice oppression and persecution in general. In some countries, citizens are prevented from traveling on the pretext of national security or political stability. For example, in some oppressive systems, political opponents are banned from traveling during periods of protests and revolutions. Restrictions on the movement of citizens are imposed to prevent the spread of new ideas and restrict individual freedom and mobility.

In modern times, communication tools, the internet, and international communication opportunities have become widespread for the world's population. However, oppressive regimes use modern intelligence tools to monitor people and control their freedom. One tactic of oppression is exploiting information technology to monitor internet content and track the digital activities of citizens. For example, in some oppressive countries, travelers' movements are monitored, and their personal information is collected through an advanced surveillance system, hindering freedom of movement and discouraging citizens from exercising their basic rights.

These examples indicate that travel and oppression are closely related, as travel still carries effects of restrictions and oppression to this day. Nevertheless, the role of travel continues to promote communication, cultural understanding, and social change. We must work together to overcome these restrictions and fight against oppression, through struggling against violations and raising awareness of the importance of the right to travel and personal freedom.

In conclusion, it can be said that travel and oppression are inseparable. While individuals seek liberation and exploration of the world through their journeys, they must also confront and resist the oppression of different systems. It is an ongoing battle that global communities must participate in, in order to achieve equality and justice and ensure the right to travel for everyone, regardless of the oppression imposed on them.
